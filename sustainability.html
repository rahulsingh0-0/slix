Slix and Sustainbility

Notable advancements in lubricant technology might potentially lower emissions and save fleet costs at the same time.

The use of biodegradable oils—whether they are synthetic or vegetable—is causing a significant change in the environment and signaling a safer substitute for the environment. Our lubricants are at the forefront of continuing advancements in lubricant innovation, distinguished by decreased flammability and lesser dangers to flora and fauna. In particular, advances in biodegradable lubricants—which also include hydraulic fluids—focus on increased biodegradability and reduced toxicity, which makes them ideal for use in activities that take place in ecologically sensitive places.

Even with these developments, there is still a serious problem with lubricant inappropriate disposal, which leads to an astounding 50% of lubricants used worldwide ending up in the environment. The main offenders that pollute vital ecosystems including fields, woods, and rivers are unintentional releases and spills. One solution that works well is to use biodegradable lubricants, which dissolve easily in water and soil and lessen their negative effects on the environment. Broadly speaking, companies that use biodegradable lubricants not only meet customer demands for sustainability, but also comply with environmental laws that require them to take responsibility for any damage done to water bodies, protected animals, or natural habitats.

A few steps we dedicatedly are taking at SLIX to contribute to sustainability
  1. Innovation and research: We are determined to develop environmentally friendly products and also reduce emissions.
  2. Promoting the use of Biodegradable substances.
  3. Promoting the proper disposal of the substances.
